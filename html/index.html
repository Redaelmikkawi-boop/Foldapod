Multistable Inflatable Origami Structures

A novel approach to deployable architecture using geometric frustration and fluidic actuation

📖 About This Project

Why This Shape?

The shelter design is inspired by the inscribed angle theorem from geometry, which allows triangular building blocks to have two compatible states: flat and deployed. This creates a structure that can:

  • ✅ Fold flat for transport (0.5m height)
  • ✅ Deploy to full height (2.3m) using air pressure
  • ✅ Remain rigid without continuous power (bistability)
  • ✅ Provide a functional door for access

The 16-face circular pattern was chosen for structural symmetry, while 2 missing faces create a door opening without compromising stability.

Design Inspiration

Based on research by Melancon et al. (Harvard University), this project scales the concept to meter-scale emergency shelters. Key innovations:

  • 🏕️ Rapid deployment for disaster relief
  • 🚚 Flat-pack transport (fits on a truck)
  • 💨 Inflation with any air pump (no complex machinery)
  • 🔒 Self-locking due to geometric incompatibility

📐 Origami Geometry Calculations

Based on the inscribed angle theorem and geometric incompatibility

Volume of a Triangular Building Block

\[ V_{ABC} = \frac{w_A \|AB\|^2}{6} \frac{\sin \alpha}{\sin(\alpha+\beta)} \sqrt{\sin^2 \beta - \frac{w_A^2}{\|AB\|^2}} \]

α (alpha)
Internal angle at vertex A (degrees)
β (beta)
Internal angle at vertex B (degrees)
||AB||
Length of edge AB (mm)
w_A
Deployment height of vertex A (mm)

Compatible Deployment Height

\[ w_A^c = \|AB\| \sqrt{1 - \frac{\cos^2 \beta}{\sin^2(\alpha + \beta)}} \]

Maximum Volume

\[ V_{ABC}^{max} = \frac{\|AB\|^3 \sin \alpha \sin^2 \beta}{12 \sin(\alpha + \beta)} \]

Geometric Incompatibility

\[ \Delta_{ABC} = \|AC_{xy}\| \cdot \sin(\alpha_{xy} - \alpha) \]

This metric quantifies the geometric frustration during deployment. When Δ = 0, the structure is at a stable state. The maximum Δ creates the energy barrier that makes the structure bistable.

Inflation Constraint

\[ \log h = \log \left( \frac{\Gamma^{V^{max}}}{\Gamma^c} \right) \geq 0 \]

This ensures the structure reaches its expanded stable state before maximum volume during inflation.

⚙️ Mechanical Engineering Design

Stress analysis, factor of safety, and material selection

Factor of Safety (FOS)

\[ \text{FOS} = \frac{\sigma_{\text{yield}}}{\sigma_{\text{max von Mises}}} \]

For our corrugated plastic structure (2mm thickness), with estimated maximum stress of 3.8 MPa and yield strength of 12 MPa:

\[ \text{FOS} = \frac{12 \text{ MPa}}{3.8 \text{ MPa}} = 3.16 \]

✓ FOS > 2.0 → Safe design

Von Mises Stress (Bending)

\[ \sigma_{\text{von Mises}} = \frac{M \cdot c}{I} \]

Where:

  • M = Bending moment (N·mm)
  • c = Distance to neutral axis (thickness/2 = 1 mm)
  • I = Moment of inertia = (b·h³)/12

Energy Analysis (Bistability)

\[ \mathcal{E} = \int p \, dV \]

The pressure-volume curve shows a negative pressure region, indicating bistability. The energy landscape has two local minima corresponding to the flat and deployed states.

🔄 Kinematics Study

Motion analysis of the deployable origami structure

Deployment Angle (Dihedral Angle)

\[ \gamma = \arccos \left( \sqrt{1 - \frac{w_A^2}{\|AB\|^2 \sin^2 \beta}} \right) \]

This angle describes the rotation between the flat and deployed states. At γ = 0°, the structure is flat. At γ = 90°, it is fully deployed.

Vertex Position Functions

\[ \text{Vertex A: } (0, 0, 0) \]

\[ \text{Vertex B: } (L \cos(\alpha+\beta), 0, -L \sin(\alpha+\beta)) \]

\[ \text{Vertex C: } (L \cos \beta, 0, L \sin \beta) \]

\[ \text{Deployed A': } (0, w_A, 0) \]

Degrees of Freedom

The structure has 1 degree of freedom during deployment (w_A). All other vertices are constrained by the geometry of the triangular faces. When assembled in a circular pattern, the structure maintains symmetry around the Y-axis.

Why Corrugated Plastic?

  • ✅ High stiffness-to-weight ratio
  • ✅ Available in large formats (8ft x 4ft)
  • ✅ Can be scored to create compliant hinges
  • ✅ Weather resistant for outdoor shelters
  • ✅ Cost-effective for prototyping
  • ✅ Recyclable and lightweight

Hinge Design

Hinges are created by scoring (removing 75% of material thickness) along fold lines. For 2mm thick sheets, the remaining thickness at hinges is approximately 0.5mm, providing adequate flexibility while maintaining structural integrity.

\[ \text{Hinge Stiffness} \propto \frac{E \cdot t_{\text{remaining}}^3}{12} \]
