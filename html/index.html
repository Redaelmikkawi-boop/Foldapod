Multistable Inflatable Origami Structures

A novel approach to deployable architecture using geometric frustration and fluidic actuation

Kinematic Design

Based on the inscribed angle theorem, our triangular building blocks achieve two stable states through geometric incompatibility.

V = \frac{w_A \|AB\|^2}{6} \frac{\sin \alpha}{\sin(\alpha+\beta)} \sqrt{\sin^2 \beta - \frac{w_A^2}{\|AB\|^2}}
💪

Structural Integrity

Stiff faces and compliant hinges minimize bending energy while allowing deployment. Von Mises stress analysis ensures factor of safety > 2.

\text{FOS} = \frac{\sigma_{\text{yield}}}{\sigma_{\text{max von Mises}}} \geq 2.0
🚪

Functional Door Mechanism

4 faces form a door that rotates 90° around a vertical hinge axis, demonstrating real-world functionality for emergency shelters.

\theta_{\text{door}} \in [0°, 90°] \quad \text{rotate around vertical hinge}

Energy Analysis

The structure exhibits bistability with an energy barrier that locks it in the deployed state after inflation.

\mathcal{E} = \int p \, dV \quad \text{with negative pressure region indicating bistability}
🔄

Circular Pattern

16 faces arranged around Y-axis with a 30° door opening (2 missing faces) for access.

\theta_{\text{step}} = \frac{360°}{16} = 22.5° \quad \text{Door gap: } 51.4°
📊

Material Selection

Corrugated plastic sheets (4mm) for meter-scale prototypes with scored hinges for compliance.

E_{\text{corrugated}} \approx 1.5 \text{ GPa} \quad \text{FOS} = 3.2
