📖 About This Project

🏗️ Why This Shape?

Inspired by the inscribed angle theorem

The shelter design allows triangular building blocks to have two compatible states: flat and deployed. This creates a structure that can:

  • ✅ Fold flat for transport (0.5m height)
  • ✅ Deploy to full height (2.3m) using air pressure
  • ✅ Remain rigid without continuous power (bistability)
  • ✅ Provide a functional door for access

🎯 Design Inspiration

Based on Harvard University research

  • 🏕️ Rapid deployment for disaster relief
  • 🚚 Flat-pack transport (fits on a truck)
  • 💨 Inflation with any air pump
  • 🔒 Self-locking due to geometric incompatibility

📐 Origami Geometry

Based on the inscribed angle theorem

📦 Volume

Triangular building block

\[ V_{ABC} = \frac{w_A \|AB\|^2}{6} \frac{\sin \alpha}{\sin(\alpha+\beta)} \sqrt{\sin^2 \beta - \frac{w_A^2}{\|AB\|^2}} \]
α, β - Internal angles
||AB|| - Edge length (949 mm)
w_A - Deployment height

📏 Compatible Height

Stable deployed state

\[ w_A^c = \|AB\| \sqrt{1 - \frac{\cos^2 \beta}{\sin^2(\alpha + \beta)}} \]
605.3 mm
Slant Edge

📈 Max Volume

Peak inflation point

\[ V_{max} = \frac{\|AB\|^3 \sin \alpha \sin^2 \beta}{12 \sin(\alpha + \beta)} \]

⚠️ Geometric Incompatibility

Energy barrier for bistability

\[ \Delta_{ABC} = \|AC_{xy}\| \cdot \sin(\alpha_{xy} - \alpha) \]

When Δ = 0 → Stable state. Maximum Δ creates the snap-through energy barrier.
