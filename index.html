Project Overview

Geometric Foundation

Inscribed angle theorem application

The shelter design utilizes triangular building blocks that exhibit two stable states: flat and deployed. This geometric property enables:

  • Flat-pack transport with 0.5m height
  • Full deployment to 2.3m using pneumatic actuation
  • Passive locking mechanism via geometric incompatibility
  • Integrated door mechanism for accessibility

Research Context

Based on Harvard University research

  • Rapid deployment systems for emergency response
  • Flat-pack logistics for efficient transportation
  • Simplified inflation using standard pneumatic sources
  • Self-locking mechanism eliminating continuous power requirements

Origami Geometry Analysis

Governing equations based on the inscribed angle theorem

Volume Formulation

Triangular building block

\[ V_{ABC} = \frac{w_A \|AB\|^2}{6} \frac{\sin \alpha}{\sin(\alpha+\beta)} \sqrt{\sin^2 \beta - \frac{w_A^2}{\|AB\|^2}} \]
α, β - Internal angles of the triangular face
‖AB‖ - Reference edge length (949 mm)
w_A - Deployment height of vertex A

Compatible Deployment Height

Stable configuration condition

\[ w_A^c = \|AB\| \sqrt{1 - \frac{\cos^2 \beta}{\sin^2(\alpha + \beta)}} \]
605.3 mm
Slant Edge Length

Maximum Volume

Peak inflation condition

\[ V_{max} = \frac{\|AB\|^3 \sin \alpha \sin^2 \beta}{12 \sin(\alpha + \beta)} \]

Geometric Incompatibility

Bistability energy barrier

\[ \Delta_{ABC} = \|AC_{xy}\| \cdot \sin(\alpha_{xy} - \alpha) \]

When Δ = 0, the structure resides at a stable equilibrium. The maximum Δ value corresponds to the snap-through energy barrier enabling bistable behavior.

Hinge Design Methodology

\[ \text{Hinge Stiffness} \propto \frac{E \cdot t_{\text{remaining}}^3}{12} \]

Scoring removes 75% of material thickness, resulting in a remaining hinge thickness of 0.5 mm, providing adequate flexibility while maintaining structural integrity.
